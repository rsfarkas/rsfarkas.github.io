    • Adventures with Benny!

    Design Question

    How can we encourage the development of soft skills in children ages 7-9?

    Concept

    In order to provide children of households with low parental involvement regardless of socioeconomic status with soft skills, Benny goes on a variety of interactive storybook adventures designed to give children autonomy, confidence in their decision making ability, and soft skills.

    Implementation

    • Sketch screen creation
    • Marvel Prototyping

    Product

    Benny's adventures take the form of interactive storybooks. When childrenâ€™s actions have purpose they are more significant. The decision making in these scenarios is very real to the child as they become personally invested in the outcome. Interactive storybooks may play an important role in children's emotional development as confidence in decision making helps boost self esteem.

    The story moves along as the user clicks to simulate page turning. When the user gets to a question screen they must make a choice regarding what action they think Benny should take. If the user selects an incorrect response they are given an explanation screen and taken back to try to help Benny solve the question again.

    The interaction between Benny and the interactive storybook is created using Processing and Arduino, with the help of the Firmata library. I decided to use littleBits, an educational platform of easy-to-use electronic building blocks for the hardware. The littleBits touch sensor placed in Benny's arm makes his "heart", another litteBits piece of hardware, light up when pressed. This interactivity establishes an emotional connection between the user and Benny.

    When a user chose the correct storyline the littleBits vibration motor in Benny's stomach goes off, indicating positive feedback. Benny is not necessary to use the interactive story book. While Benny helps to create an emotional connection, not being able to play without him would limit the use cases

    The first adventure created for Benny was developed for the iPhone, as this is the most common technology kids ages 7-9 have access to.
